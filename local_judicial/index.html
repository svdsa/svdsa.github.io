Local Judicial Politics

Overview

On the local level, Judges are elected to six year terms. They are locally divided into two court houses: Santa Clara County (sixth district; 89 Justices) and San Mateo County (first district; 29 Justices).

Elections

The Attorney General is elected for four year terms (and the current term ends in 2018), with a cap of two such terms. Santa Clara County provides information on when its judges will face re-election.

How Judicial Policy is Formed

The policy-making body of California courts is the judicial council headed by the Chief Justice of California. The council has 6-8 business meeting which normally allow for public comments (unless stated otherwise). Additional information about how the courts function can be found at: www.courts.ca.gov (including listing of task forces and public meetings).

How can judges be removed from office?

The Commission on Judicial Performance investigates complaints made against judges of the Superior Court, the Court of Appeal, and the Supreme Court. Further, the Commission accepts written Public complaints (with no support for online submissions). The address and guidelines for complaints can be found at cjp.ca.gov/file_a_complaint/ . If found to be guilty of judicial misconduct, the commission can discipline a judge.

Further, Judges can be recalled if an amount of signatures is collected that surpasses twenty percent of the votes cast for the judge in the last election. And if the judge had not appeared on the last election, recall signatures need to equal twenty percent “of votes cast for whichever countywide office received the least total number of votes in the most recent general election in the judge's county”.
