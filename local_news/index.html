Local News Recommendations

  • The best general sources for news in Silicon Valley are:
    the San Jose Mercury and the Silicon Valley Business Journal . Good Twitter sources are @408Nate on new construction, @CALmatters on California policies, and @JuliaCarrieW on technology. Boom Magazine (@BoomCalifornia) is a very good California-based magazine.
  • In terms of housing, good follows are:
    @KimMaiCutler (a VC and former journalist), @MBridegram (an SF-based lawyer), @Urban_habitat (an advocacy org), @SVatHome (an advocacy org), and @Transform_alert (an advocacy org focused on transit).
  • For a better understanding of alternative economies in the Bay Area:
    Oakland-based @TheSELC is a good follow. The South Bay Labor Council, @sobaylabor, also has good information.
  • To keep up to date on California's health care activism:
    follow @CalNurses.
  • For understanding Bay Area water issues:
    Stanford's Water in the West program (@WaterintheWest, run by @FelicityBarr) is a good source of information for local environmental issues. At the Sacramento Bee, @RyanSabalow looks at the Central Valley, which is where much of the Bay Area water comes from.
  • For understanding the region's prison problems:
    we recommend Critical Resistance, Prison Activist, and Black & Pink .
  • For LGBTQ resources:
    San Francisco's Bay Area Reporter is a great local resource and is the longest-published LGBTQ newspaper in the US. San Jose's Billy DeFrank Center is Silicon Valley's LGBTQ Community Center. @PFlag (and its San Jose chapter) are also a great source of information.
